The Digital Land team and policymakers at the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG) have built a national dataset of brownfield land. We collect brownfield land data from local planning authorities and run it through our pipeline process, which updates the data to meet the standard and fixes common errors in the data.

Whilst our process has significantly improved the data, we cannot guarantee its accuracy, since it relies on the data published by 345 organisations.

After collecting and processing the data, we’re able to build a national map of brownfield land and calculate important numbers for housing and planning, such as the number of potential dwellings that could be built on this land.

Our work builds on the National Housing Federation’s brownfield map and CPRE’s research into the state of brownfield land. We’re not aiming to duplicate this work, but want to contribute to this national picture by making the data easier to find, use and trust.

Our relationship with local planning authorities means we’re able to improve the data by:

  • improving and simplifying the data standard
  • working closely with many local planning authorities, in particular through research and feedback sessions
  • changing and improving policy, for example introducing financial incentives for publishing data that meets the standard

Our map and calculations are just an example of how this data can be used. We hope that the data will support innovative research, the development of digital services in the PropTech sector, and other projects that will improve planning and housing.

Download the data

After collecting the data, we run it through our pipeline process. This changes the original data published by local planning authorities so that it meets the data standard, as well as correcting some common errors.

We recommend that you download the processed data. However, all original datasets published by local planning authorities are also available.

Download the processed data as:

  • Geojson
  • CSV file
  • Data package
